Cookie policy

Last updated: 15 April 2026

This policy explains how aml-store.com (the “Site”) uses cookies and similar technologies, consistent with your choices in the cookie banner and with Google’s Consent Mode v2 when Google tags are present on the Site.

Data controller

Aml Store — VAT 11461870963 — email: Info@amlstore.it.

What cookies are

Cookies are small text files that websites and third-party services may store on your device while you browse. Similar technologies (for example web storage) may be used for comparable purposes.

Preferences stored on your device

Your choices about non-essential cookies are stored in the browser’s localStorage (technical key aml-consent-v2) to avoid repeatedly showing the banner and to restore consent signals when pages load. You can clear them at any time in your browser settings.

Google Consent Mode v2

The Site loads an initial script that sets the default consent state according to Google Consent Mode v2: categories related to advertising measurement and analytics are set to “denied” until you consent. After a valid choice, the state is updated so any Google tags (for example Google Analytics or Google Ads) can respect your preferences. If those tags are not installed, the script does not start measurement on its own.

Consent types handled include, for example: analytics_storage, ad_storage, ad_user_data, ad_personalization, functionality_storage, personalization_storage, plus security_storage (always active for essential security needs).

Cookie categories

  • Strictly necessary: required for the Site to work (for example security, load balancing, session or basic preference handling). They do not require consent.
  • Functionality and personalization: improve features and personalization; enabled only if you select the related options in the banner.
  • Measurement (statistics): help us understand how the Site is used; enabled only with consent.
  • Marketing and advertising: may be used for ads and campaign measurement; enabled only with consent.

Payment services and third parties

If you use payment features (for example Stripe), the provider may set its own cookies or identifiers under its own policy. Please read the provider’s notice at checkout.

How to manage or change preferences

You can review your choices at any time using “Manage cookie preferences” in the footer or the button below.

Updates

We may update this cookie policy for legal or technical reasons. The date at the top of the page shows the latest revision.
